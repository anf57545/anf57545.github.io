One of the most important facts about Louisville is how to pronounce the city properly. The city is named after King Louis XVI and most visitors attempt the city as Louis-ville. There is a Louisville, Georgia and Colorado that is pronounce the way that you would think, LOU-iss-vill. However, as a person born and raised in Louisville, I know that that is not correct. It is in fact pronounced as if you had marbles in your mouth. To put it into text is a bit difficult, when I am asked where I am from, I try and enunciate which sounds like loo-a-vul, but when I am just speaking it turns into sounding luh-vul. I know I sound uneducated to most people who have never heard of this rule, but I have no interest in changing it. I feel that is it more acceptable than the pronunciation of Versailles, Kentucky (look it up).
